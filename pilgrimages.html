BUDHISM PILGRIMAGES

Mahabodi Temple,Bodh gaya

The Mahabodhi Temple (literally: "Great Awakening Temple") or the Mahabodhi Mahavihar, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is an ancient, but much rebuilt and restored, Buddhist temple in Bodh Gaya, marking the location where the Buddha is said to have attained enlightenment. Bodh Gaya (in Gaya district) is about 96 km (60 mi) from Patna, Bihar state, India. The site contain a descented of the bodhi tree under which Budha gained enlightement ,and has been a major pilgrimages destination for not only budhhism but for Hindu also for over past two thousand year.

Budha statue

One of the most famous religious sites in bodh gaya,Bihar is the massive statue of the great Gautam Budha.The Great Buddha Statue also known as 80 feet statue is in Bodhgaya. The unveiling and consecration of the Great Buddha Statue took place on 18 November 1989. The consecration ceremony was attended by the 14th Dalai Lama, who blessed the 25-meter statue, the first great Buddha ever built in the history of India. Statue is now a symbol of the holy place Bodhgaya, next to Mahabodhi Temple which is a World Heritage site, and enjoys constant visits of pilgrims from all over the world. Among local people, it is nicknamed "the 80-foot (25-meter) Buddha Statue."

Vishwa Shanti Stupa

Vishwa Shanti Stupa (English: World Peace Stupa) is a large white stupa in Rajgir, Nalanda District, Bihar, near Gitai Mandir. Statues of the Buddha are mounted on stupa in four directions. It also has a small Japanese Buddhist temple with a large park. There is a temple near the stupa where prayers are conducted for universal peace. Located on about the top of 400 m high Ratnagiri Hills in the lush-green valley of Rajgir, the magnificent white stupa stands 120 ft tall with a total diameter of 103 ft. The stupa is studded with four gold gilded majestic statues of Buddha, showing four important events of his life.

SIKH PILGRIMAGES

Patna Sahib Gurudwra,Patna

It is major pilgrimages destination of sikhism. Patna is a major pilgrimages to sikh pilgrimage as it reckoned to the birth place of last sikh guru , GURU GOVIND SINGH. Takht Sri Harmandir Sahib Ji, also known as Patna Saheb is a prominent tourist attraction in Patna. A sacred place for Sikhs all over the globe, Patna Saheb was built by Maharaja Ranjit Singh from 1837 to 1839. This shrine is dedicated to Guru Gobind Singh ji, the tenth Guru of the Sikhs who was born in Patna back in 1666. One of the most iconic figures in Indian history, Guru Gobind Singh has a legacy that can make anyone feel modest. The gurdwara faced a massive earthquake in 1934 so new constructions started in 1954. This gurdwara is home to many holy Sikh scriptures. The shrine is very beautiful to look at and you can feel the power of people’s faith the moment you enter it.
